% Problem 1
\problem Find a solution to the system of linear equations given below and then verify that the solution is correct.
	\[
	\begin{cases}
	2x - y= 4 \\
	4x + y= 8
	\end{cases}
	\] \pspace

\ans{Using elimination, observe that by adding the equations, we have\dots
	\begin{table}[!ht]
	\centering
	\begin{tabular}{rrrrrr}
	& $2x$ & $-$ & $y$ & $=$ & $4$ \\
	$+$ & $4x$ & $+$ & $y$ & $=$ & $8$ \\ \hline
	& $6x$ & $+$ & $0$ & $=$ & $12$ \\ 
	\end{tabular}
	\end{table}
But then $6x= 12$, which implies that $x= 2$. Using this in the second equation, we have\dots
	\[
	\begin{gathered}
	4x + y= 8 \\
	4(2) + y= 8 \\
	8 + y= 8 \\
	y= 0
	\end{gathered}
	\]
Therefore, the solution is $(x, y)= (2, 0)$, i.e. $x= 2$ and $y= 0$. \pspace

Alternatively, using substitution, we can solve for $y$ in the first equation:
	\[
	\begin{gathered}
	2x - y= 4 \\
	2x= y + 4 \\
	2x - 4= y \\
	y= 2x - 4
	\end{gathered}
	\]
Using this in the second equation, we have\dots
	\[
	\begin{gathered}
	4x + y= 8 \\
	4x + (2x - 4)= 8 \\
	6x - 4= 8 \\
	6x= 12 \\
	x= 2
	\end{gathered}
	\]
But then $y= 2x - 4= 2(2) - 4= 4 - 4= 0$. Therefore, the solution is $(x, y)= (2, 0)$, i.e. $x= 2$ and $y= 0$. \pspace

We verify this by checking that it satisfies both equations:
	\[
	\begin{aligned}
	2x - y&= 4 &\qquad\qquad 4x + y&= 8 \\[-0.1cm]
	2(2) - 0 &\stackrel{?}{=} 4 & 4(2) + 0 &\stackrel{?}{=} 8 \\[-0.1cm]
	4 - 0 &\stackrel{?}{=} 4 & 8 + 0 &\stackrel{?}{=} 8 \\[-0.1cm]
	4&= 4 & 8&= 8
	\end{aligned}
	\]
}
